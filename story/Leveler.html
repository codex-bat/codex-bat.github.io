Leveler

📅 July 8, 2026
Leveler, they call him Kna 3iseR fyt

⠀ ⠀ ⠀The otherwordly cathedral rang with an eerie hum. The air was heavy with fine, damp dust that stuck to the throat --- yet it almost felt empty. The walls were cracked and brutal, un-defiable decay has taken its toll on them. The white tinted glass let in a strange navy blue glow --- a light cast by the Ophus moon. The devoted members of this Holy Establishment were faced with their own, impending demise. They feared the most that Their downfall will be used as an example. They feared... what They did not yet know --- what They have only heard of. The Raze was approaching. What or who was The Raze, They were not aware of. In fact, They knew very little. But They were soon to find out as distant thuds grew louder and louder. Chains clinked and clanked as The Raze drew near with heavy emphasis. Each step shook the ground to its core, dust flying off of the surface they previously rested on.

⠀ ⠀ ⠀The steps grew louder with each passing minute until suddenly, the gates of the cathedral were forced open by the powerful burst of wind. The idealized and ritualized sense of solitude was broken by the grayscale, rocky environment leaking in from beyond the gates. The Raze stood there, a deadly saber held in a single, tight grip. It just... stood there. It had no visible facial features, but All knew it wasn't good --- it was dark. Its face dripped with something blood-red, but it wasn't blood itself... it was something else. Its silhouette was distorted by the deformed muscles it carried. It smelled of rotten flesh and repugnant musk. It couldn't have a voice. No longer was it anything other than The Raze.

⠀ ⠀ ⠀None of The Servants wanted to approach it. None, but one --- a stupid act of bravery, if They knew what that meant. The Servant with the designation 84 approached the looming doom; what It hoped to achieve, It didn't know Itself. Before It could even say a "word", The Raze twisted its wrist and swung with such devastating power that it could lift a blade forged across eons. The Servant's bowels fell apart, limping onto the floor as Its upper body was severed from the lower one. Its eyes were still focused on an object no longer in front of It, unable to process what has happened to It. Its mouth still open as the body parts slammed against the floor, blood spreading around them. Screams could be heard --- violent, high-pitched screams of hysteria and madness. But it didn't come from The Servants, no. It was the mirage of the unknown forces The Raze harboured. It instilled fear in The Servants as They realized, it was inescapable. They wanted to run. They should've ran, but Their duties bound Them to the hall. The very hall that now wore spilled blood like pain. The sight was... horrifying. The long hall was now a room of despair and violence as The Raze continued towards its objective. The Servants tried to stop it, to no avail. Some froze. In awe, in shock, in admiration or uncertainty --- it's unknown. One Servant, drenched in a Fellow's warmth, could not look away from the way the moonlight caught the freshly damp stones. The Raze gripped Them by their skull and crashed them into mere particles while maintaining its slow, terrifying pace. Its chains --- rattling --- embedded its sound and texture to the very consciousness of each servant as They faced their timely end. It was in those few last seconds they realized why the legends spoke, and even forbid, the chains. The chains were the tool of intimidation. For those still alive, The Raze was no longer just a distant power that looms; it was now The Judge of The Living. Perhaps it always was.

⠀ ⠀ ⠀Screams. Dust. Blood. With every power, with every thrust of the sword, with every violent exertion: the chapel lost more of its "purity". The floors and walls filled with blood that clung to them like a baby to a mother. The only sounds capable of leaving The Raze's structure were the painful, heavy groans formed from the Godly exertions. They tried punching it, but its unfathomable muscle structure diffused every punch, every sonic scream, every single effort made. Everything was bound to perish against it: effort, motivation, will, power, hunger, resentment, hatred, joy, hearing, morale, even duty. A Servant opened Their mouth wide, letting a Lautatherian sonic sound rupture through space like shock through body. It didn't even phase the glaring entity. It instead set its unknown sights on The Servant. The Servant's face contorted --- almost as if it was melting --- as It realized it didn't even phase the monster standing in front of It. A bloodcurdling realization hit Them: all servants were witnesses to its judgement on them --- or so They thought. Perhaps Their scriptures misrepresented it. Perhaps Their scriptures misjudged it. What does it all matter in the wake of death? It was not here to learn of Them. It was here to make sure They could no longer convert the ones worthy of the greatest pleasures of knowledge.
