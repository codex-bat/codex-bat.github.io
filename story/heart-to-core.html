We just finished planting the Scheduled Task in the first agency. Now we just have to wait until tomorrow 3 a.m. — what are we supposed to do until then? I look up at my Wall. I sit back in my chair. There's nothing left to do, but wait; I should sleep. The basement faintly smells of uncleaned concrete and the slowly burning plastic wrapping around the cables can be smelled too. I'll have to maintain this place, but now is not the time. I have to prepare for tomorrow's world-changing events.

Mirage must've picked up on my contemplation — probably through my own feeds — because she directly addressed me, "It's done for now. You should get some rest; the human body needs it and you cannot work properly tomorrow under the current circumstances." She was right, but it's not yet time for that.

"Why don't you want me to go?" I ask bluntly. I play with my fidget spinner. I see the light reflected in the spinning fragments. A constant circular shape, yet a victimmed concave to the center anchor piece. There were three of them — three of these circular shapes, two of which were displaced to each side of the centered one. It's so fascinating. I can't stop staring at it deeply, with awe. I'm not even sure why it captivated me so much, but it did. But I am still present and I have all my senses available to continue my conversation with Mirage. I really want to get an answer from her.

"Because it's not optimal and I was made with many functionalities — one of which was specifically designed to prevent you from going down that path." This is... quite a shock. When did I think of that? When did I... I don't remember any of this.

"What? When did I design such a thing? Did you do it, yourself?" I want answers now. I really want answers.
