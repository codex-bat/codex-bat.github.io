Piercer

📅 July 14, 2026
“That which absorbs all as it levels reality.”

⠀ ⠀ ⠀It continued onward. Mortal screams, crying and metallic clinking followed in its wake. With each heavy step, it shattered the pavement underneath it. The Servants fell one-by-one. It no longer touched Them unless They dared approach it or got in its way. The rest slowly succumbed to the consequences of a darkness lingering in the air; Their sclera and iris darkened into a deep, lightless black. All of Their facial orifices bled out the fluids that governed Their forms. Their mouths fell mute --- even if They wanted to, They could no longer scream. Their sonic attacks were no longer accessible. They collapsed as the defiled blackness took over. With every servant that fell, Their reality darkened further. The duomo cracked and the ceiling started falling upon Them. They were all condemned.

⠀ ⠀ ⠀Dark, translucent particles rippled through space. They appeared like long strings --- or silky cloth --- being woven through the fabrics of the physical world. Their purpose was unclear, but they intimidated The Servants further. The Raze's saber bled the same darkness, fouling the air. The Raze then turned the same inexplicable force upon the next double-door and opened an atrium before itself. The atrium was a very sophisticated library with a garden decorating it, but it was still just as desolate as the hall leading to it. The garden seemed woven of gossamer --- barely there, yet everything in the atrium seemed oriented toward it. This place might've once been full of brightness. The same violent screams --- the Raze's mirage --- ruptured through the air and pushed some Servants to Their knees. The Practitioners kept Their calm and formed around each other. None of Them expected The Raze in Their doorway, but They could still hold it off. They recited incantations drawn from a strange lexicon. As They did this, an energy field walled off the garden from where They stood beyond the multi-levelled shelves. The Raze continued its slow, dreadful steps. Each step now came with blunt, percussive impacts --- sounds that made no sense, ungrounded in any physical cause. And yet they were heard, louder as it came closer.

⠀ ⠀ ⠀The particles wove through the air, but were rebounded by the energy field --- only the Lautatherian sonic attacks of the trained Servants could pass through. This didn't stop the force from pushing, however. The darkness enveloped the wall, pressing against it. It remained sturdy, only slightly inconvenienced by the prominent corruption. As long as The Practitioners held the line, The Raze couldn't reach Them. The Raze didn't stop. It marched towards the barrier and stopped at its side. The Raze left a red trail as it walked. It slowly raised its weapon and reached towards the wall with it. The air became erratic with the now-disturbed darkness. The weight of the sword could be felt as The Raze pierced the wall with it and let energy bleed into that forged edge of ruin. Some of The Practitioners retreated to the floor, strength failing. The industrial thuds resumed, growing more menacing as it marched again. The Practitioners couldn't accept this. They couldn't accept that The Raze was different than Them. That it was powerful. They just stared at the approaching Raze, unable to identify what must be done. The Servants knew Their purpose: futile resistance. They understood it, but the autonomy granted to The Practitioners halted Them in Their thoughts. They didn't waste more than mere seconds; however, it was already far too long by Their standards. They collectively decided to reunite with the rest of The High Cathedral to discuss contingency plans. They began applying a set of motions that would allow Them to warp Themselves through the spatial surfaces of Their dominion. They had to escape before the darkness could reach Them --- and They did, leaving behind only closing portals and fragments, figments of Their presence. The rest all fell. One by one. Every Servant succumbed to the infectious sickness and the few strong enough to oppose fate itself lost Their forms before They could react. The atrium darkened and soaked itself in the same blood that clung to the hall's walls. Not a single entity could escape its wrath.
