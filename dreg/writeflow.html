writeflow

📅 July 8, 2026

I made two writings in the past two days. for each, I had something that set the mood and gave me the idea.

this was always common to me: listening to or watching anything would always spike up my creativity, however lately I've been actually capturing these moments to write down what I had. I'm not sure if I expect myself to become a professional writer (even just as a side-thing), but it's helpful. I'm being productive in some way, without the endless frustrations of hardware and software limitations. this is also one of my joys with coding, it's almost never a problem with my equipment and more so my lack of awareness on documentation.

with music it's different. it's in the middle. I can make mock-ups and concepts super easily, but when a project becomes overly complex, I reach the ceiling. and I dislike bouncing, because I most of the time I need dynamic control for revisions. with editing and visual stuff it's A FUCKING NIGHTMARE. Davinci is unreliable with layers and effects, because even the slightest resolution shift can cause ridiculous star-bugs. Blender is awesome and I can work on it, but again... I reach a ceiling. I'm not optimizing well, but it shouldn't be that bad... well regardless, I'm not even talking about the rendering stage, because I almost never reach it. I'm almost always stuck trying to finish my scenes, but never can because of constant crashes. I watch several tutorials on different things that would help populate my scene or materials (I am a sucker for procedural materials, but... it's the bottleneck for my PC. it's a luxury I can't afford. literally can't afford), but actually applying those learnt things becomes hard when my omen can no longer handle it.

holy rant, anyway- this dreg was supposed to reveal the songs I used for each work. untitled's concept popped up in my head with everything it entailed when feverishly, I was listening to virus 1.0 and virus 2.0. when coming up with the name, Mirage was Miriam or Myriad (for a myriad of reasons --- haha funny stfu... im kidding). the rest of the names came on the spot, but with good reason. Leveler birthed itself through a gutting urge to write it down immediately when this (ofc it's Evilwave) came up. my background experience with DOOM: Eternal and DOOM as a whole was a strong influence for sure, but I'm still glad I managed to capture a similar atmosphere within my own writing.

hope you enjoy!
