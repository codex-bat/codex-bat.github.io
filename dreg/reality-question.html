reality?

📅 July 22, 2026

I went to sleep at 8PM yesterday for a quick nap. it was the scariest shit. I felt fever-ish. I was cold even though I was sweating from heat just earlier in the day and I throat and eyes weren't alright. yk, the usual stuff. I drool when I sleep. it's something that started happening a few months ago. but now it was especially scary, because it felt like the past few days with playwright weren't real. I was questioning whether or not the ambitious projects I've been working on were real or not... projects like the elevator that I won't say much about for now ;3

it was scary. I thought maybe she was a figment of my imagination and somehow she's died years ago and I've been compartmentalizing for years of my life. the thought, the idea, felt so real. it felt like I've been living a lie and i'm just now realizing what the truth is and what my brain has hidden from me. and there was no substantial evidence to believe this other than the shock from playwright 2.0 or the associate not being home on Friday and the never-subsiding stress from the past few days since. somehow I was almost convinced, but then confused on whether it was real. I didn't want to fall asleep out of fear of losing my mind --- it felt like it was slipping away from my control.

I could see a beauty in it, but it was definitely scary. I kept trying to think of... naughty thoughts to distract myself, but lately I've been noticing i'm often struggling to keep my thoughts going. I backtrack and have to think to continue what I was trying to say, because I get distracted and quickly forget. it worries me a bit, but there's just so much going on... I just really hope and think it might be the stress accumulating. I hope my flight will be alright. redoubt is low-key excited about it. I'm worried. whenever I see an airplane, I think of how it feels to be in it, up there... each turn feels like the plane is taking 45 degrees and seeing the earth below us is... not pleasant at all (terrifying). Xanax helps, but it just makes me feel sleepy and I'm so worried of falling asleep. each bump --- whenever turbulance hits --- jolts me awake or at the very least makes me check the window for the wings. I've been flying since I was a kid at ~9-11 years of age, yet it still happens. I don't get it. I cannot overcome it and I don't know why or how.

I'll come back soon, see you for now;
