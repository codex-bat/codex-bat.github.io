servility

📅 June 24, 2026

# DISCLAIMER: SPOILERS FOR MY SCI-FI GAME PLEASE CONTINUE WITH DISCRETION.

I made a whole post ranting about subservience, but I'm realizing that's not what a title such as "servility" could be used well in. if you're interested in that rant, you can find it right here.

Bothuner knows what it means to serve. he served many in many ways. he was made to complete a mission no one was sure could be done. he was made to act according to guidelines set upon him. he was made to serve humans in a way or another. not literally, but in the sense that he feels the obligation for it. morals? in a robot? not literally, they're enforced by the guidelines. and he doesn't try to go against them or question them. it'd be irrational and all reasoning points to not going against them, for his own good.

that's not the only way people can perceive him as a servant. in the religious subroute of Fastflow [...], Bothunter comes upon a cult-like integration. they're rare survivors of the harsh environments (or perhaps their remnants, not sure), who have him to thank for it. this is when our bot's identity is developped further, most prominently, as the past slowly gets unravelled. it turns out they worship him as some sort of god-sent messiah or archangel of sorts. he's been here before, but he doesn't remember. his first memory only leads back to the lab he left once he was prepared for the mission. perhaps he's been here before...? perhaps this isn't his first attemp. how else would these "cultists" know of his model and design?
