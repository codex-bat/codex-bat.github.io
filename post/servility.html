servility

📅 June 24, 2026

# DISCLAIMER: SPOILERS FOR MY SCI-FI GAME PLEASE CONTINUE WITH DISCRETION.

I made a whole post ranting about subservience, but I'm realizing that's not what a title such as "servility" could be used well in. if you're interested in that rant, you can find it right here.

Bothuner knows what it means to serve. he served many in many ways. he was made to complete a mission no one was sure could be done. he was made to act according to guidelines set upon him. in a sense, you could perhaps argue he was made to serve humanity's survival; those few left. not literally, but in the sense that he was programmed that way. he underwent thorough psychological training, unaware, to promise a succesful misison. this naturally comes with the "good morals" that humans describe and you might even wonder "morals? in a robot?" not literally, no. they're enforced by the guidelines and he doesn't try to go against them or question them. why should he anyway? it'd be irrational to do so and all reasoning points to not going against them, for his own good.

but please do not misunderstand, he doesn't serve for the sake of helping out. he doesn't help out anyone. he doesn't side with anyone. he doesn't empathise for anyone. he only knows one thing: he needs to reach the focal point of the Umbra Project — a capsule swarm of solar-reflective nanomaterial seeded with a plasma-reactive compound (‘Helion-9’) [...]. that's what all of his actions revolve around. and he's been prepared for the presence of the other automated machines that'll try to stop him.

however, back to the topic of today's post: that's not the only way people can perceive him as a servant. in the religious subroute of Fastflow [...], Bothunter comes upon a cult-like integration. they're rare survivors of the harsh environments (or perhaps their remnants, not sure), who have him to thank for it. this is when our bot's identity is developped further, most prominently, as the past slowly gets unravelled. it turns out they worship him as some sort of god-sent messiah or archangel of sorts. he's been here before, but he doesn't remember. his first memory only leads back to the lab he left once he was prepared for the mission. perhaps he's been here before...? perhaps this isn't his first attemp. how else would these "cultists" know of his model and design?

he doesn't remember. that's the central them of Fastflow. what about the book? he doesn't reme--- who knows :3. as tragic as it is, this is his story. all of this.
