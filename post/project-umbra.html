Detailed Description

Fastflow is a passion project designed as a compact yet rich entry point into a larger universe. Set in a near-future dystopia where Earth's surface is scorched and toxic, humanity has escaped aboard last-minute mega-ships, leaving behind AI-driven robots once tasked with fixing the climate. Without direction, the machines reinterpret their purpose, concluding that the sun’s solar radiation must be decreased to save what's left.

You play as the Bothunter, emerging from an underground non-human fallback shelter. Your mission is to stop the machines from executing their plan to make the sun less harmful (more in-depth explanation in The Sun in All of This section), even if success means Earth's total annihilation is still inevitable. Along the way, your choices—though limited—will influence side-paths and unlockable endings, offering replayability and achievement incentives.

The gameplay loop emphasizes relentless movement and tactical combat. Mechanics include:

  • Grapple Mobility: Inspired by DOOM Eternal's meathook but redesigned as a standalone tool with cooldown.
  • Momentum-Based Traversal: Weapons like shotguns or launchers can be used for self-propulsion, enabling advanced movement tech. This can pair well with explosive elements such as barrels lying around, although at the cost of some Health Points.
  • Ricochet Shots: A coin-flip-inspired (ULTRAKILL) bullet-throw mechanic lets you shoot airborne bullets mid-flight, bouncing between enemies for devastating effect.
  • Unique Arsenal: Modern, futuristic SMGs, heavy shotguns, and a late-game railgun-style weapon with a powerful cooldown-based blast.
  • Trial Mode (Training Grounds): A focused combat challenge and endless mode to prototype core mechanics early on with minimal art overhead.
  • The Trials: A less-focused set of levels to further prototype core mechanics on controlled map-layouts midway through with more assets and sprites.

Stylistically, Fastflow leans into detailed pixel animations akin to Hollow Knight, with tight controls and weighty feedback for every shot and dash. While not a full RPG, your journey will be affected by small branching decisions, rewarding exploration and player style.

Fastflow is part of a broader narrative universe, my developing Opdolpha, but it stands entirely on its own. It subtly plants seeds for deeper connections without requiring the player to know the full lore. Cameo appearances like ... serve as fun Easter eggs rather than core plot elements.

This game is my way of sharpening my dev skills and building reusable assets and music for future titles. While music is currently my top priority, Fastflow lays the foundation for my eventual dive into more expansive storytelling and game design—making it a meaningful prologue to something bigger, even if relatively small in scope.

The Sun in All of This

⠀ ⠀⚙️ The Setup

  • The bots’ mission:

⠀ ⠀“Reduce solar radiation to safe human levels. Repair ozone depletion.”

  • Their solution:

⠀ ⠀Launch ‘Project Umbra’ — a capsule swarm of solar-reflective nanomaterial seeded with a plasma-reactive compound (‘Helion-9’).

⠀ ⠀Helion-9 wasn’t chemically stable. When introduced into an environment with intense proton flux, it catalyzes runaway magnetic reconnection events — essentially amplifying solar flares by orders of magnitude.

⠀ ⠀⚛️ What Helion-9 Is

⠀ ⠀Helion-9 was designed to bind with the Sun’s coronal plasma, forming a thin reflective layer to dim harmful radiation output (altering solar albedo).

—

⠀ ⠀Helion-9 is a synthetic photonic plasma stabilizer — a nanostructured compound designed to regulate solar fusion output by resonating with the sun’s magnetic field. Its intended use: “calm” solar flares and reduce UV output — an attempt to fix the ozone crisis by tuning the sun itself.

⠀ ⠀But… it’s unstable. Once enough Helion-9 saturates a localized region of the corona, it can invert magnetic polarity—triggering a magnetic reconnection cascade across the solar surface. That’s the event Umbra-7 detects: a reconnection wave powerful enough to dump terajoules of magnetic energy at once, blowing the sun’s outer layers into a flare bright enough to bleach the Earth within seconds. So when the capsule carrying Helion-9 hits the right plasma density, you’ll get a blinding flash — a flare that might not explode the sun entirely (like I had naively planned) but ionizes the atmosphere. Seconds later, the charged particle wave (CME NASA ↗️, WIKIPEDIA ↗️, ESA ↗️) arrives, frying every electrical circuit, boiling the upper atmosphere, and igniting orbital debris. It’s quick, tragic, and realistic.

⠀ ⠀This is basically a direct casual link to the bots, as their cold calculations were too short-sighted and lacked proper research.

⠀ ⠀🌍 Aftermath of All of This

⠀ ⠀Even if Bothunter is SEU-hardened (resistant to cosmic rays and radiation bit flips), a solar flare of this magnitude is beyond survivable limits.

⠀ ⠀He might stay functional just long enough to transmit something — a message, a warning, a goodbye — before his systems fry under hard X-ray exposure. That’s scientifically grounded and narratively devastating.

⠀ ⠀🌐 The Ozone and Satellites - Supporting Context

  • Thousands of small satellites (Starlink, comms, weather) reentering the atmosphere do release alumina and chlorine compounds.
  • Those destroy ozone by catalyzing reactions that strip O₃ molecules — similar to the old CFC problem.
  • Combined with decades of geoengineering projects (carbon mirrors, reflective aerosols, etc.), Earth’s upper atmosphere could already be thin and unstable.

⠀ ⠀So when Helion-9 destabilizes the sun, the ozone layer collapses almost instantly, UV radiation floods the surface, and within minutes, most of the biosphere is gone.
